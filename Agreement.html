Section 1

🌱 Singular & Plural Subjects

⭐ The Golden Rule
Singular subjects take singular verbs. Plural subjects take plural verbs.
  • Earline attends college. (singular)
  • That boy delivers newspapers. (singular)
  • They attend college. (plural)
  • Those boys deliver newspapers. (plural)
📝 Verb Phrases
The first helping verb agrees with the subject.
• Earline is attending college, but they are attending.
• A boy has been delivering newspapers; two boys have been delivering.
📝 Phrases Following the Subject
The number of a subject is NOT determined by a word in a phrase that follows it.
• A book of poems is on the shelf. (book is the subject)
• Together with, in addition to, as well as, along with are prepositions — they do NOT affect number.
📝 Negative Constructions
A negative construction after the subject does NOT change its number.
• Carl, not Juan and I, is doing the artwork.

🔮 Indefinite Pronouns

Indefinite pronouns don't refer to a specific person or thing. Knowing whether they are singular, plural, or both helps you pick the right verb!

Singular (always)Plural (always)Both (it depends!)
anyone, anybody, anything
everyone, everybody, everything
no one, nobody, nothing
someone, somebody, something
one, each, either, neither		both, many, several, few, othersany, all, more, most, none, some
⭐ Singular
Singular indefinite pronouns take singular verbs.
  • Neither of the animals has been fed.
  • Somebody is bringing a CD player.
  • One of the puppies has chewed a hole in your shoe.
⭐ Plural
Plural indefinite pronouns take plural verbs.
  • Both of them have been informed.
  • Several of the women are pilots.
📝 The "Both Ways" Group (any, all, more, most, none, some)
Look at the object of the prepositional phrase after the pronoun.
• All of the pizza was eaten. (pizza = singular)
• None of the students were able to open the PowerPoint. (students = plural)
📝 Either / Neither — Three Roles
• Neither animal has been fed. (adjective)
• Neither the cows nor the pigs have been fed. (conjunction)
• Neither of the animals has been fed. (subject → singular verb)

Section 3

🌸 Compound Subjects

🌿 Joined by AND

  • Usually → plural verb
  • Ramon and she like hiking.
  • Her brother, uncle, and cousin are teachers.

🌸 AND — Exception!

  • Names ONE thing → singular
  • The secretary and treasurer was Eduardo.
  • Rhythm and blues has become our favorite.
⭐ OR / NOR
Singular + singular → singular verb
Plural + plural → plural verb
  • Marcelo or Donya knows the address. (both singular)
  • Neither phone nor doorbell was working. (both singular)
  • Do the Wilsons or the Campbells live there? (both plural)
📝 Mixed Singular + Plural with OR/NOR
The verb agrees with the subject closest to the verb.
• Either Harry or his aunts are planning activities. (aunts = closer = plural)
• Neither the potatoes nor the roast is done. (roast = closer = singular)

Section 4

🦋 Special Problems in S/V Agreement

⭐ Collective Nouns
Singular (group as unit) or Plural (members individually) — context decides!
  • The class has met its substitute. (one unit → singular)
  • The class were disagreeing with one another. (individuals → plural)
⭐ Predicate Nominatives
A verb agrees with its subject, NOT the predicate nominative.
  • The main ingredient in salsa is tomatoes.
  • Tomatoes are the main ingredient in salsa.
⭐ Inverted Sentences (subject follows verb)
Find the real subject — then make the verb agree with IT.
  • Here is a set of keys. / Here are the keys.
  • Does he know the price? / Do they know?
📝 Expressions of Amount
• One unit → singular: "Two years is a long time."
• Separate parts → plural: "Two years (2006 and 2008) were rainy."
• Measurement → generally singular: "Three cups of flour is what the recipe requires."
📝 Singular Nouns That Look Plural
Physics, linguistics, news, mathematics → always singular verbs.
• Physics is a science. • News was disappointing.
📝 Titles, Countries, Cities, Organizations
Even if plural in form → singular verb.
• The Hunger Games is the first book. • Carrier Computers is having a sale.
📝 Every / Many a(n)
Takes a singular verb.
• Every parent and grandparent is looking on proudly.
• Many a hopeful performer has gone to Broadway.

Section 5

🌼 Pronoun/Antecedent Agreement

The antecedent is the noun or pronoun that a pronoun refers back to. Pronouns must agree in number!

⭐ Basic Rule
Singular antecedents → singular pronouns. Plural antecedents → plural pronouns.
  • John Green wrote many novels. His most famous is The Fault in Our Stars.
  • Many of the actors already know their lines.
⭐ Indefinite Pronoun Antecedents
Singular → singular pronoun. Plural → plural pronoun. Unknown gender → his or her.
  • Each of the teams had its mascot at the game.
  • Everyone in the club has paid his or her dues.
  • Both candidates stated their positions.

🌿 AND → plural pronoun

  • The guide and ranger placed their ponchos.

🌸 OR/NOR → singular pronoun

  • Neither Heidi nor Beth took her umbrella.
📝 Collective Nouns
• Group as unit → singular pronoun: "The orchestra was looking forward to performing its rendition."
• Members individually → plural: "The orchestra were tuning their instruments."
📝 Titles, Countries & Plural-Looking Singulars
• Have you read The Hunger Games? It was written by Suzanne Collins.
• I have good news. Would you like to hear it?
